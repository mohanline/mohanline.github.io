About Mohan

He signs his work simply Mohan. Contrast to its meaning, enamourment, allurement, fascination and a magical charm to bewilder, his line is so economical with the tiniest hint of a decorative flourish. Idealistic and pragmatic, secluded and social - Mohan has a rapier eye and also critical pen. He is cartoonist, caricaturist, illustrator, painter, a pioneer of animation in Andhra Pradesh and a shrewd writer, authoring a couple of books. Splitting off swollen bladders of fibs and vanity, cutting people down to size are what Mohan is all about.

Mohan’s exceptional minimalist style, coupled with caustic wit and astute political analysis, make him well known name since late 1970s in Andhra Pradesh, India. His long years as a journalist and as an artist left a significant mark which continues to be the signature of remarkable style in line and work that inspired many a cartoonist. His cartoons made complex issues seem simple and moral choices clear. They focused on important issues of the time, particularly making Telugus take note of the human folly that is politics.

Mohan, since his early beginning as a political cartoonist in the celebrated Visalandhra daily from Andhra Pradesh, is also an art critic who wrote extensively on all the great modern fathers of Far East and also Far Western art. Mohan and Telugu literature have been inseparable for thirty years, as a self described “characterist,” his signature work appeared in virtually every major publication, as well as numerous book covers of the last three decades. His style of writing in Telugu, his effortless strokes in ink and colour won admiration from artists, master writers, poets and critics of literature. His vast number of illustrations, cover pages, posters, cartoon strips have earned him a place and he is widely quoted by scholars across the fields of interest. In his works the scholars catch more than a glimmer of the fierce determination. It is said unanimously that immense creativity required to bring forth from such an elegant line, so unique and expressive it has come to define an artist and his art form.

Yet for Mohan there has always been a world beyond world of letters, exciting his passions and focusing his vision, producing witty, stylized cartoons, caricatures, paintings, animations supporting social reform. His sketches established an innovative visual language of modern graphic line, which reemphasizes his professional roots and personal interests, putting his genius into context.

Politicians of all parties, columnists like Mani Sankar Iyer, cartoonists like Abu Abraham preferred Mohan for their writings. Years of his refined work proved him to be ‘one man army’ of first ever animation from Hyderabad, where he has been residing since three decades.
